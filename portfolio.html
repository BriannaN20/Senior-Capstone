Detailed Documentation of Process & Artifacts

How we moved from initial textbook concepts to a live public health digital platform.

Artifact A: Interactive Presentation

Our first core artifact was our research deck. We used it to break down complex medical literature on toxic food dyes and chemical preservatives into structural, digestible profiles before displaying them to the community.

Artifact B: Video Grid & Portal

Our second artifact was our medical video database. By embedding raw testimony from healthcare professionals alongside a live feedback form, we transformed a basic report into a real platform for change.

Our Methodological Journey: We began by thoroughly reviewing clinical literature on food additives like Red 40 and Sodium Benzoate. From there, we structured primary data points by coordinating video interviews with local healthcare practitioners. Finally, we compiled our literature synthesis, clinical data, and public outreach forms together into a responsive front-end application deployed on GitHub Pages.

Overcoming Challenges & Friction

No major project happens without roadblocks. Our greatest obstacle during development was translating dense clinical data regarding pediatric metabolic health and behavioral trends into communication that the general public could understand. Early drafts felt far too textbook-heavy and exclusive.

!

The Critical Pivot: We realized that to actually promote community change, we needed to stop writing like medical textbook copyists and start acting like community educators. We stripped away heavy academic jargon and replaced it with an interactive, scan-friendly chemical monitoring table and direct key insights underneath our media files.

Logistically, we also faced real constraints coordinating calendars with active medical workers to capture our video interviews, which taught us valuable professional lessons in communication, asset management, and timelines.

Key Learning Moments & Personal Growth

The turning point of this entire capstone experience occurred when we analyzed our active feedback loop dashboard. Seeing that a definitive 72.7% of community respondents pledged to swap out chemical-heavy snacks for cleaner alternatives was a massive realization. It proved to us that public health advocacy is not a passive field—when you deliver accurate, clear, and highly accessible data, people will actively alter their daily health habits.

On a personal level, this research trajectory completely changed how we evaluate commercial nutritional packaging and significantly built our confidence in handling professional clinical data and primary interview coordination.
